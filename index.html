Kindesbrunnen

Tibor Köhne, 2004
Digitalized 8mm film, mini-beamer projection on floor, wooden tower covered with metal foil, ca. 30 cm x 200 cm x 30 cm

The video installation Kindesbrunnen takes the historic dark green-lacquered iron water pumps that can be found on every other street corner in Berlin as a witness of the past - playing with the water from these pumps is an inseparable part of a Berlin childhood on hot summer days. In the video work, supposedly old images of a youth merge with current images of the artist, suggesting childhood memories from long ago. The memories of the pump pour out of it and result in a flood of blurred and distorted childhood images on the floor. Crooked and playful, ultimately childlike, the installation opens up questions about memory and its distortion. The digitized 8 mm material blurs the boundaries between now and then.

Phina Hansen, 1999 - Portland
Video, Polylactic acid

Some people are made of plastic. Selfies and Tiktok dances, more and more the social media using generation performs for a global public. But what are we really showing, is it honest? With an increasingly visual culture, the “real love” that we display or attempt to attract is reduced to what we can see. A disconnect which feels like dancing with your back to someone. Bodyscape III, 2023

Mathilda Augart & Carlos Martín, 1993 - Dresden, 1996 - Madrid
Super8 film in wooden box

The installation explores the subtle interplay between our external environment and the depths of the unconscious. But this film goes beyond the conventional narrative, inviting the viewer to traverse the inner landscapes of a body. Directed and filmed by the duo Carlos Martín and Mathilda Augart, ‘Bodyscape III’ reveals fragments of their lives. Objects from the artists' personal histories appear in the visual narrative, blurring the boundaries between reality and reverie. Set against the backdrop of Berlin, the film is both a portrait of the place and a gateway to the inner mysteries of its creators. Music by Thomas Ankersmit

Pommersche Kosmonautenschafe, 2023

Anna Eigner, 1999
Super 8 film 2023 Drawings, oil pastel and soft pastel on fabric, 2024

EN In Anna Eigner's artistic work, she examines everyday life in immediate and personal environments through drawing and documentation. The results of her observations reveal contradictions, grievances and the unusual. For Kosmonauten Schafe, her living environment has been a major theme. The outskirts and the city in which she lives not only influences her biography, but also inform her themes. In this work Eigner tries to explore how people, flora and fauna live and coexist in the peripheral areas of the city. How do we interact? How do we see each other? To gain a better understanding of her environment, Eigner studied a flock of sheep in her neighbourhood. DE In ihrer künstlerischen Arbeit untersucht Anna Eigner zeichnerisch und dokumentarisch das alltägliche Leben im unmittelbaren und persönlichen Umfeld. Die Ergebnisse ihrer Beobachtungen offenbaren Widersprüche, Missstände und Ungewöhnliches. Für die Kosmonauten Schafe ist ihr Lebensumfeld ein wichtiges Thema. Das Randgebiet und die Stadt, in der sie lebt, beeinflussen nicht nur ihre Biografie, sondern prägen auch ihre Themen. In dieser Arbeit versucht Eigner zu erkunden, wie Menschen, Flora und Fauna in den Randgebieten der Stadt leben und zusammenleben. Wie interagieren wir? Wie sehen wir uns gegenseitig? Um ihre Umgebung besser zu verstehen, beobachtete Eigner eine Schafherde in ihrer Nachbarschaft.
